Animals
Wildlife encompasses the diverse array of animals and plants that inhabit natural ecosystems around the world. These species play crucial roles in maintaining the balance of nature, contributing to the health and sustainability of their habitats. Protecting wildlife is essential for preserving biodiversity and the intricate
web of life on Earth.

Squirrels are agile and resourceful rodents, recognized for their bushy tails and ability to swiftly navigate treetops. They play an important role in forest ecosystems by aiding in seed dispersal.

Elephant
Elephants are the largest land animals, celebrated for their intelligence, strong social bonds, and remarkable memory. Their presence is vital for the ecological health of their habitats, as they help shape landscapes and disperse seeds.

Seal
The harp seal pup is famous for its soft, white fur that camouflages perfectly against the snowy Arctic landscape. These adorable marine mammals rely on their thick coat for warmth in the frigid temperatures. As they grow, their fur changes color, and they become adept swimmers in the icy waters.

Puma
Pumas, also known as cougars or mountain lions, are elusive and adaptable predators found across the Americas. They are solitary hunters, known for their agility and strength, able to take down prey much larger than themselves.
